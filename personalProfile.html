Tactile

If you are a tactile learner, you learn by touching and doing. You understand and remember things through physical movement. You are a "hands-on" learner who prefers to touch, move, build, or draw what you learn, and you tend to learn better when some type of physical activity is involved. You need to be active and take frequent breaks, you often speak with your hands and with gestures, and you may have difficulty sitting still. As a tactile learner, you like to take things apart and put things together, and you tend to find reasons to tinker or move around when you become bored. You may be very well coordinated and have good athletic ability. You can easily remember things that were done but may have difficulty remembering what you saw or heard in the process. You often communicate by touching, and you appreciate physically expressed forms of encouragement, such as a pat on the back.
(What's Your Learning Style? 20 Questions, 2020)

What This All Means

Most of the results of these three online tests rings true to me. I need to set plans in place to have small achievable goals that I can mark off as I progress. I know already that making plans to make plans irritates me, and almost immediate direction is what I need, however, I need to be mindful of the other team members’ methods. I will have to read each of my teammates profiles to better understand them and the way they operate. With regards to the learning style test, I perform better with hands-on experience. This is supported by my drive for self learning, that may be outside the scope of what is prescribed. Finally, with the big 5 personality test, the key takeaway is that I am more confident and secure (less neurotic), which fits in with my willingness to ask for feedback on subjects that I am not fully educated on.
